How hour calculation works in My Clock
My Clock was developed to reproduce the company's calculation rules as faithfully as possible, allowing each employee to track their hours worked, overtime, and estimated gross pay.

Below is an explanation of how calculations are performed within the app.

1. Shift start time
Each employee has an official shift start time, which may vary by team or sector.
Examples: 04:00, 07:30, 13:00, 16:00. This time is the main basis for calculating hours worked.

2. Clock-in rule
The system always considers: the shift start time or the actual entry time, whichever is later.
This means:
- if you arrive before your scheduled time, no extra pay is generated.
- if you arrive late, the calculation starts from your actual entry time.

3. Clock-out rule
Upon exit, every minute worked is counted normally. There is no rounding.

4. Unpaid interval (meal break)
The minimum unpaid break is 30 minutes. The system deducts 30 minutes or the actual break time, whichever is greater.

5. Overtime calculation
Overtime is calculated on a weekly, not daily, basis. The main basis considers the total hours worked from Monday to Friday.

6. Normal hours
The first 39 hours worked in the week are considered normal hours.

7. Overtime A (1.5x)
After completing 39 hours in the week: the next 4 hours are paid at 1.5x.

8. Overtime B (2.0x)
Everything exceeding 43 weekly hours is paid at 2.0x (double time).

9. Saturday
Saturday counts as a continuation of the week. Depending on the total accumulated, it can be Overtime A or B.

10. Sunday
All hours worked on Sunday are paid as Overtime B (2.0x) from the first minute.

11. Final result
The app shows daily/weekly totals and estimated gross pay.

Important: My Clock is a support tool. The official company system and internal rules always prevail.
